Timeline of Lorenzo de'Medici

1449

Lorenzo de'Medici was born in Florence in January 1449 to Piero de'Medici and Lucrezia Tornabouni.

June 1469

Lorenzo de'Medici marries Clarice Orsini, in an attempt to obtain political power with the Orsini family and their connections with the Papacy.

December 1469

Upon the death of Piero de'Medici, Lorenzo ascends to power in Florence as de-facto rulers of the republic.

1478-1480

The Pazzi family and Pope Sixtus IV attempt to assassinate the Medici family, killing Lorezo's brother, Giuliano.

1490

Lorenzo de'Medici appoints Francesco Savonarola to the convent of San Marco.

1492

Lorenzo de'Medici died at the longtime family villa of Careggi on the 8th April 1492.

Who is Lorenzo de'Medici?

Early Life

  • Lorenzo de'Medici was born in Florence. His father and grandfather, Piero and Cosimo de'Medici respectively, were de facto rulers of the Florentine Republic. As such, it was intended that Lorenzo would become the ruler after the death of Piero.
  • Lorenzo was educated by some of the great thinkers of the Renaissance, such as Gentile de'Becchi and Marsilio Ficino. Moreover, Piero would send him on diplomatic missions to meet religious and political figures.
  • In 1469, Lorenzo married Clarice Orsini and throughout their marriage, they had 10 children, one (Giovanni de'Medici) would become Pope Leo X.
  • At the age of 20, Lorenzo's father died of gout and he became the de facto ruler of Florence. However, some families, such as the Pazzi believed that Lorenzo was too young and inexperienced to rule Florence.

Leader

  • Lorenzo intended to rule in a similar manner to his grandfather, Cosimo de'Medici, using constitutional methods. However, Francesco Guicciardini defined his regime as "that of a benevolent tyrant in a constitutional republic."
  • Lorenzo spent a large amount of his time trying to instill a peace along the peninsula of Italy. He was an advocate of the Treaty of Lodi (1454) which provided a political alliance between the Duchy of Milan, the Republic of Venice and the Republic of Florence.
  • As a result of the political alliances between the Northern states in Italy, Pope Sixtus IV became increasingly concerned about the power that Lorenzo was gaining. In order to diminish his power, he apponted his nephew to work with the Pazzi family. Girolamo Riario and Francesco de'Pazzi conspired to assassinate Lorenzo and his brother, Giuliano, in the catherdral at the Easter mass. Giuliano was murdered on the 26th April 1478 and Lorenzo was wounded while he was escaping.
  • As a result of the failed assassination attempt, troubles between Florence and the Papacy increased and led to the continued war between Venice, Florence and Milan against the Papacy and Naples. Moreover, this strenghtened the Medici families power basis, which would ultimately lead to the Medici family becoming Dukes of Florence.
