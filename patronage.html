Patronage

Andrea del Verrocchio

Verrochio was commissioned by the Medici family for a number of artistic works and sculptures. Moreover, he often taught young, upcoming artists and sculptors, most notably Da Vinci, Ghirlandaio and Botticelli, all of whom had a profound influence upon the Renaissance artistic movement and who would themselves go on to receive commissions from the Medici family.

Verrochio was commissioned by the Medici family to sculpt a bronze statue of David. It was purchased by the signoria in 1476.

Alessandro Botticelli

There are various connections between Botticelli and Lorenzo de'Medici. Although, it was previously believed that Lorenzo was a patron of Botticelli and commissioned various works of his, it is now widely accepted that Lorenzo's patronage of the artist was a recommendation to other wealthy patricians to commission his work. Nonetheless, Botticelli was a close friend of the Medici, and often incorporated them into his art.

The adoration of the Magi painted around 1475 was commissioned by Gaspare di Zanobi, features many members of the Medici family, including Cosimo (the elder), Piero (Lorenzo's father) and Lorenzo de'Medici himself.

Michelangelo

Michelangelo has become synonymous with the Renaissance and went on to become a leading artist during the time, painting some of the most famous works of art, such as the Sistine Chapel in the Vatican. From 1490-1492, Michelangelo attended the Platonic Academy, a humanist academy founded by the Medici. He was commissioned by Lorenzo to sculpt the Battle of the Centaurs (1491-1492).
